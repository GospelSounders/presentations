How the Shift Occurred

The Mervyn Maxwell Influence

The shift began with scholars like Mervyn Maxwell introducing the concept that:

  • Christ is both Judge AND Advocate simultaneously
  • God needs to "justify His ways" to created beings
  • Saints will "examine God's ways and judgment"

Maxwell's Teaching: "Jesus, our Attorney and Judge... If, then, Jesus plays a dual role in the judgment, serving both as our advocate and as our judge..."

The theological shift can be traced to specific scholars, particularly Mervyn Maxwell, whose book "God Cares" introduced several problematic concepts: 1. Christ as both Judge and Advocate (which Ellen White clearly says is NOT the case) 2. The idea that God needs to justify Himself to creatures 3. The concept of "theodicy" - justifying God's ways to men From Maxwell's "God Cares": "They will confirm to their eternal satisfaction how earnestly and patiently God cared for lost sinners. They will perceive how heedlessly and stubbornly sinners spurned and rejected His love." This language suggests saints need to be "convinced" of God's fairness - which directly contradicts Ellen White's statement that "none might question the justice of the decisions made." The influence spread through "Seventh-day Adventists Believe..." (1988) and then into Sabbath School lessons.

The Pagan Origins of "Theodicy"

Theodicy Definition:

"The question of how God could be all-loving, all-powerful, and all-knowing yet allow evil to continue to exist"

From Greek Philosophy (Epicurus 341-270 BC):

"If God is willing to prevent evil, but not able, then he is not omnipotent. If God is able to prevent evil, but not willing, then he is malevolent. If God is both willing and able, then why is there evil?"

Uriah Smith's Warning:

"No theodicy ever devised in the pagan world has ever made any such provision."
The concept driving the modern millennial judgment teaching is "theodicy" - a term coined by Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in 1710, but the concept goes back to pagan Greek philosophy. Cliff Goldstein (who promotes the modern view) openly admits this is theodicy: "What's amazing about theodicy isn't so much that God can, ultimately, justify His ways before us, but that He would consent to do so." This is fundamentally flawed because: 1. It assumes God needs to justify Himself to creatures 2. It promotes human reason as judge over divine wisdom 3. It's rooted in pagan philosophy, not biblical revelation 4. It contradicts the biblical principle that we walk by faith, not sight Uriah Smith contrasted pagan theodicy with biblical revelation: "It is not the teaching of revelation that there should be an attempt to counteract the sorrows of bereavement by mere human stoicism; but the sorrow is to be mollified by the hope of the gospel." The solution is not philosophical explanations but gospel faith. Ellen White never used the term "theodicy" in all her writings - the angels never chose that word for her.
